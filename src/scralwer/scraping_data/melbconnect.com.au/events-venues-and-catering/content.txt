Event Venues
Would you like to host your next meeting, conference, reception, workshop, seminar or exhibition at Melbourne Connect?Click here for event enquiries or download the venue guide. See below for information about catering for meetings and events.

























                The Superfloor
            

                The 3,600sqm Superfloor sets a new global benchmark for innovation community curation and partner amenity. A dedicated collaboration space that connects the Precinct, the Superfloor is accessible to all colocated partners and has been designed by leading architect Woods Bagot.
            






















                Meeting Rooms
            

                The precinct is home to 16 bookable meeting rooms ranging in size, from 4 pax rooms to a 22 pax boardroom, all equipped with AV and VC as well as on-site services to suit all occasions, including beverages (water and barista coffee), catering (morning tea through to hot meals), technical support and workshop/meeting equipment hire.
            






















                The Forum
            

                The Forum is a highly versatile space located alongside the entrance to the Superfloor that can host 400 (lecture), 200 (dining), or even be split into three smaller rooms with a 100-person capacity for events such as seminars, presentations and workshops.
            






















                The Launch Pad
            

                The Launch Pad is a dynamic space used for creating, prototyping, pitching and testing, it is the hotspot of the Superfloor with an ever-changing canvas of events and activations. It showcases a central open plan area to facilitate ideation and knowledge sharing between precinct tenants, the University, guests and the business sector.
            






















                The Studio
            

                The Studio is a high-traffic space with ground floor exposure at Melbourne Connect, this 70-person tiered seating space is a lively area that hosts workshops, hackathons, group activities, and has a “war room” for project work.
            






















                Womin-djerring
            

                An open air oculus known as womin-djerring (come together) sits in the centre of Melbourne Connect. Surrounded by laneways, this space connects the public to the precinct.
            






















                Science Gallery Theatre
            

                Down the stairs from the main gallery sits the Science Gallery Theatre; a multi-purpose theatre space with state-of-the-art audio visual equipment, and an in-house AV team that can support complex technical events.
            






















                Science Gallery, Ground Floor Galleries
            

                Science Gallery Melbourne is located on the Ground Floor of Melbourne Connect. The Ground Floor houses lofty gallery spaces on the east and west side of the building, which are connected via an entrance foyer and café area.
            



























Event and Meeting Room Catering
